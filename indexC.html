Saving Energy (Electricity) Saving Water Miscellaneous

Turn off lights:

Turning of lights when not in use

Turning the lights off (Along with any other appliance), when not in use is a simple way to save energy and reduce energy bills. By incorporating this habit, households can contribute to lower carbon emissions and greater environmental conservation, alongside seeing a decrease in their utility expenses.

Unplug Appliances when not in use:

Unplugging Appliances when not in use

Unplugging appliances when they are not in use can significantly reduce energy consumption and lower electricity bills. Many devices, even when turned off, continue to draw small amounts of power, a phenomenon known as "standby power" or "phantom load." This includes common household items like televisions, game consoles, computers, and chargers.

Use Solar Energy:

Use Solar Energy

This is a very common technique which is also very much used in the Netherlands. Solar panels can be installed on the roof and absorb the sunlight to convert it into energy. It is also possible to buy little solar panels and place them in your garden to get more sustainable energy.

Be careful when to wash clothes:

Be Careful when to wash clothes

There are certain factors you should consider while washing clothes again. To save energy, it is recommended to wait till you have enough clothes to wash so your machine is full. Another way is to wash with cold water. This can save you up to 106 euros per year. Drying clothes in a dryer wastes a lot of energy. It is recommended to let them dry outside in the summer and unplug the dryer.

Use a dishwasher:

Use a dishwasher

When you use the dishwasher, it is possible to save up to 24 gallons of water in one wash, rather than wasting more water washing the dishes manually. This can save you up to 5000 gallons of water in a year. This can also save you up to 100 euros a year on utility bills.

Utilise used water:

Utilise used water

Utilizing used water, or water reuse, is an effective strategy to save energy by reducing the demand for new water supplies and the associated energy-intensive processes of extraction, treatment, and transportation. Reclaimed wastewater and gray water can be repurposed for various non-potable applications such as irrigation, industrial processes, and toilet flushing. This reduces the need for fresh potable water, conserving the energy required for its treatment and distribution.

Take short showers:

Take short showers

Taking shorter showers is an effective way to save energy. By reducing the duration of your shower, you decrease the amount of hot water used, which in turn reduces the energy required to heat that water. This simple change can lead to significant energy savings over time. For instance, shortening your shower from eight minutes to four minutes can save around 36 liters of water per day.

Replace or check your Air Filters:

Replace or check your Air Filters

During the hot season (or any other season for that matter), you should check your air filter once a month to prevent them from becoming clogged. If the air filter is dirty and not changed, the system requires more energy to cycle the air and therefore wastes energy.

Close doors and windows not in use:

Make sure to close doors and windows if not needed

When your heater is running on gas and you want to keep the house warm while keeping the monthly bill low, you should avoid leaving windows or doors open for too long. When windows are left open too long, the warm air escapes, causing the heater to work harder to maintain the desired temperature. This increased workload requires more gas, leading to a higher gas bill at the end of the month.
